📖 GreenFarm INTL — Dashboard Documentation

This dashboard visualizes all 76 inventory transactions from the GreenFarm INTL Inventory Master Ledger, covering April 2025 to March 2026 across two countries (Malaysia & Philippines), five product categories, and 26+ clients. All monetary values are taken strictly from Excel Column Q (Total Value) — 19 rows with missing unit prices show ₱0 and are never estimated. Total IN: ₱9.98M · Total OUT: ₱5.63M · Grand Total: ₱15.61M.

⚠️ Data Notes & Cleaning Logic

📋 All 76 Rows Included

All 76 ledger rows are included — none excluded. However, 19 rows have Total Value = ₱0 in Excel Column Q because no Unit Price was entered. These are real stock movements (quantities exist, prices pending). They count in transaction totals and quantity charts but contribute ₱0 to all value-based KPIs. No values were estimated or fabricated.

Total IN: ₱9,976,000 · Total OUT: ₱5,630,832 · Grand: ₱15,606,832

📅 Date Correction Applied

9 date cells were stored as Excel date serials (datetime objects) instead of text strings. Excel misread ambiguous DD/MM/YYYY entries — e.g., "03/11/2026" (3rd Nov) was stored as the serial for November 3 instead of 3 November. All 9 affected dates have been corrected by swapping day/month: the Nov 3 2026 record (KIM ENRIQUEZ) is actually 11 Mar 2026, confirmed by its position in the running stock sequence.

Corrected: 2026-11-03 → 2026-03-11 · 2026-08-03 → 2026-03-08 · etc.

🏷️ Transaction Types

Stock IN = procurement / goods received from suppliers (increases inventory). Stock OUT = sales / dispatches to clients (decreases inventory). Only rows with a defined IN or OUT type are included.

Valid types: "IN" | "OUT" only

💱 Currency

All monetary values are in Philippine Peso (₱). Values ≥ ₱1,000,000 are displayed as M (millions), ≥ ₱1,000 as K (thousands), for readability in KPIs and chart axes.

Display: ₱9.98M = ₱9,976,000

📌 KPI Definitions

🟢 Total Procurement (IN)

Sum of Total Value (₱) for all Stock IN transactions in the filtered view. Represents total goods purchased or received into inventory.

SUM(Total Value) WHERE TxnType = "IN"

🔴 Total Sales (OUT)

Sum of Total Value (₱) for all Stock OUT transactions. Represents the total value of goods sold or dispatched to clients.

SUM(Total Value) WHERE TxnType = "OUT"

🔵 Net Stock Value

The difference between total IN and total OUT value. A positive number means more value came in than went out — indicating healthy procurement vs. sales balance. Displayed in green if positive, red if negative.

SUM(IN Value) − SUM(OUT Value)

🟡 Total Transactions

Count of all ledger rows in the current filtered view. Each row represents one stock movement event (one IN or OUT entry).

COUNT(Rows) in filtered dataset

🟣 Unique Clients

Count of distinct client/supplier names in OUT transactions only. Represents the active customer base generating sales revenue.

DISTINCT COUNT(Client) WHERE TxnType = "OUT"

🟠 Avg Unit Price

Average unit price across all filtered transactions (both IN and OUT). Helps monitor pricing trends — a rising average may signal inflation in sourcing costs or premium product mix shift.

AVG(Unit Price ₱) across all filtered rows

🟢 Top Category

The product category with the highest combined total value (IN + OUT) in the current filter view. Identifies the primary revenue/procurement driver.

MAX(SUM(Total Value)) GROUP BY Category

🔵 Sell-Through Rate

What percentage of procured quantity has been sold (based on units, not value — since 19 IN rows have ₱0 value, unit-based rate is more reliable). A 11% rate means most stock is still held — GreenFarm is in an accumulation phase with large unsold Cashew inventory.

SUM(QtyOUT) ÷ SUM(QtyIN) × 100 [unit-based]

📈 Chart Explanations

Monthly IN vs OUT Bar

Grouped bar chart comparing total Stock IN value (green) vs Stock OUT value (red) per calendar month. Gaps between months indicate no transactions that month.

IN vs OUT Donut

High-level split of total procurement value vs total sales value as a percentage doughnut. Quick visual for overall inventory direction.

Cumulative Stock Value

Running total lines for IN and OUT over time. The gap between the two lines at any point = net retained stock value. Widening gap = more being bought than sold.

Transaction Count by Month

How many individual ledger entries occurred per month — independent of value. High count months indicate busy operational periods.

Running Stock Level

The "Running Stock" column from the ledger plotted over time. Shows the unit balance trajectory. Upward slope = net accumulation; downward = net depletion.

Revenue by Category

Horizontal bar showing total value (IN + OUT combined) per category. Cashew dominates due to high unit prices (₱7,900–₱8,620/box).

Category Share Pie

Percentage contribution of each category to total value. Useful for portfolio concentration risk — if one category is >80%, the business is highly dependent on it.

IN vs OUT by Category

Grouped bar per category showing procurement vs sales split. A tall IN bar with low OUT means stock is being held (Cashew). High OUT vs IN means that category is being run down.

Top Clients by Sales

Top 8 clients ranked by total OUT value they've purchased. DANNY and RYAN are the top two revenue clients by a significant margin.

Top Products by Value

Products ranked by combined transaction value. WS1 (Premium Cashew W-S1) leads due to its high unit price and large shipment volumes.

Transaction Count by Client

How frequently each client appears in OUT records. High frequency + lower value = regular small buyers. Low frequency + high value = bulk/wholesale buyers.

IN Value by Country

Doughnut showing ₱ procurement split by country. Malaysia = ₱9.98M (all priced). Philippines = ₱0 — not a data error, unit prices simply haven't been entered for PH shipments yet. Use the Qty chart to see PH volume.

IN Quantity by Country

Doughnut showing unit volume procured per country — unaffected by missing prices. Philippines received 4,900 units across 19 IN transactions (WS1, WS2, WW320, SP, Mangoes, Coffee, Jackfruit) compared to Malaysia's 1,420 units. PH is actually the larger procurement market by volume.

OUT Value by Country

Doughnut showing ₱ sales value by country. Useful to compare where revenue is being generated once prices are filled in for PH shipments.

IN vs OUT Qty by Country

Grouped bar comparing total units received (green) vs dispatched (red) per country. Clearly shows Philippines has far higher IN volume than OUT — large inventory is being held there. Malaysia's OUT exceeds its documented IN for some products due to cross-country stock movement.

Warehouse Distribution

Transaction count split between Main Warehouse and Cold Storage. Cold Storage is used for perishable items (Mango, Veggies); Main Warehouse for Cashew, Coffee.

Unit Price Distribution

Histogram bucketing unit prices into 6 ranges. Shows the pricing spread — whether the product mix is concentrated in low, mid, or high price points.

📦 Inventory — Remaining Stock Charts

Stock Remaining by Product

Horizontal bar showing units remaining per product (QtyIN − QtyOUT). Color-coded: green = healthy stock, yellow = low stock (<50 units), red = negative (sold more than received — data gap). Responds to all filters.

Estimated Stock Value

Remaining units multiplied by the last known unit price for that product. Only shows products where a unit price exists in the ledger. Products with ₱0 price (COFFEE12caps, COFFEE6caps) are excluded from this chart. Negative stock products are shown as ₱0.

Stock by Category (Donut)

Aggregates remaining units across all products within each category. Cashew dominates due to large unpriced IN shipments (WS1 2,605 units, WS2 1,554 units). Negative stocks are floored to 0 for this view.

IN vs OUT Qty by Product

Grouped bar comparing total quantity received (green) vs total quantity dispatched (red) per product, for the top 10 by QtyIN. A tall green bar with small red = large unsold inventory. Equal bars = fully sold. Red taller than green = data gap (negative stock).

Stock Sufficiency %

Percentage of procured stock still on hand: (QtyIN − QtyOUT) ÷ QtyIN × 100. Only products with QtyIN > 0 are shown. Color: green ≥ 70% (slow-moving / held), yellow 30–70% (active turnover), red < 30% (near depletion). Products with 100% = no sales recorded yet.

⚠️ Inventory Note: Three products — COFFEE-6, VEGGIES, W320 — show negative remaining stock. This means OUT transactions were recorded without matching IN entries in the ledger. These are either data entry gaps or the procurement records exist outside this worksheet. Investigate before reporting stock levels for these products.

🎛️ Filter Reference

📅 Date Range

Filters all charts and KPIs to show only transactions between the selected From and To dates. Defaults to full data range (Apr 2, 2025 – Nov 3, 2026).

🌍 Country

Multi-select dropdown. Malaysia = primary procurement hub (Cashew IN). Philippines = primary sales market (client OUT transactions).

📦 Category

Filter by product category: Cashew, Mango, Coffee, Veggies, Jackfruit. Deselecting a category removes all its rows from every KPI and chart.

🛒 Product

Granular filter by individual product SKU (e.g., WS1, MANGO-1KG, COFFEE-12). Useful for single-product deep dives.

🧑‍💼 Agent

Filter by the sales/operations agent who logged the transaction. Currently Florence handles Malaysia-based entries; Philippines entries have no assigned agent.

🏭 Warehouse

Main Warehouse (ambient storage, Cashew/Coffee) vs Cold Storage (perishables: Mango, Veggies). Affects which stock movements appear.

🔄 Transaction Type

Show only IN (procurement), only OUT (sales), or both. Useful for reviewing just the buying side or selling side independently.

💰 Total Value Range

Min/Max filter on the Total Value (₱) of each row. Use to isolate large bulk orders (e.g., > ₱500,000) or small spot sales.

🏷️ Unit Price Range

Min/Max filter on Unit Price per box/unit. Useful for comparing premium vs standard grade products within a category.

⚠️ Important: All filters are connected — changing any filter updates ALL KPIs, ALL charts, and the transaction table simultaneously. Click ✅ Apply Filters to confirm changes.
